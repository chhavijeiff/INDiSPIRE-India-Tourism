Beauty of Himachal Pardesh

The diverse landscape of Himachal has everything to enthrall and attract a pool of tourists. The mighty mountain peaks, deep ravines, abundance of greenery, vast flowery meadow, hazy Woodlands, golden valleys, garrulous waterfalls, icy rivers, pristine glaciers, enchanting lakes, etc.Himachal Pradesh is a haven for travelers, trekkers, and pilgrims.

Shimla

Shimla is famous for its stunning natural beauty, colonial architecture, and old-world charm. There are many places that command picturesque views of the sun rising or setting amidst the hill range at the horizon.The Shimla Ridge, in the heart of the hill town, provides stunning views of the neighbouring snow-capped Himalayas. The Shimla sightseeing tour would be lacking without visiting the Ridge, which is a large open area. Visit this location in Shimla if you enjoy admiring the majestic Himalayan Alps.

Manali

Manali is a place where the beauty of nature leaves you in awe.Manali is not just a destination, it's a paradise for adventure enthusiasts.The peace and serenity of Manali can heal the soul and rejuvenate the spirit.

Dharamshala

Located in Western Himachal region, Dharamshala is a prominent name in Buddhist tour to the state, as the home of Dalai Lama and all the Tibetans in exile. It is a well-known centre for studying Buddhism, its sutras, tantras, and major texts related to this.

Dalhousie

Dalhousie is famous for being home to the pristine and serene Dhauladhar Mountain Range. It is also famous for its Victorian architecture, colonial charm, picturesque landscapes, and adventurous activities.

Kasauli

Kasauli is famous for its many attractions like the Central Research Institute, Christ Church, Kasauli Brewery, and Sunset Point as well as various activities like trekking, shopping, and even paragliding.Greatly tourists like to travel to Kasauli in April, May and June to enjoy pleasantly warm days and cold nights! Even October, November, December, January, February and March is a preferred time to visit as its a shoulder season offering chilly but enjoyable weather for tourists.

Kufri

Kufri is a tiny hill station in Himachal Pradesh, famous for its trekking and hiking trails. In winter, adventure-seeking travelers visit Kufri to enjoy skiing and tobogganing on snow-covered slopes.

Khajjiar

Khajjiar is about 95kms from Pathankot Railway Station and 130 kms from the Gaggal Airport in the district Kangra. Khajjiar is famous for the popular Khajji Naga shrine dedicated to the serpent god from which the name is believed to have been derived.
