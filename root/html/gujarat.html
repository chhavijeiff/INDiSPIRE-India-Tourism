Beauty of Gujarat

Gujarat is a land of great contrasts stretching from the seasonal salt deserts of kachchh to the wet , fertile southeastern parts of the state. It is the one and the only place to view pure Asiatic Lions in the world.

It has ancient caves, stupas, temples that exhibit the style which blends elements of Islamic and Hindu architechture. The capital of Gujarat , Ahemdabad is India's first UNESCO accredited World Heritage City.


Statue of Unity

The Statue of Unity is the sculpture of the Iron Man of India 'Sardar Vallabh Bhai Patel'. It is situated in the Gujarat province of India confronting Sardar Sarovar Dam .It is globally recognised as the ' World's Tallest Statue '. It is a patriotic symbol of the legacy of the man who spent his life in the struggle for freedom.


Gir National Park

Gir National Park and Wildlife Sanctuary, also known as Sasan Gir, is a forest, national park, and wildlife sanctuary near Talala Gir in Gujarat, India.At present, Gir forests of Gujarat is the only place where Asiatic lions are found in its natural habitat.


Zarwani Waterfalls

Zarwani waterfall (Dheerkhadi Dhodh) is one of the best waterfalls in Gujarat and it is located near the statue of unity.It is situated inside the Shoolpaneshwar Wildlife Sanctuary which is home to a gamut of flora & fauna.

Sabarmati Ashram

Sabarmati Ashram is located in the Ahmedabad at the bank of River Sabarmati.The ashram now has a museum, the Gandhi Smarak Sangrahalaya. This had originally been located in Hridaya Kunj, Gandhi's own cottage in the ashram.

Rann of Kachchh

The Rann of Kachchh is a salt marshy land in the Thar Desert in the Kachchh district of western Gujarat.The Rann Utsav takes place during the winter months, from October to February. This is really the best time to visit the Rann of Kutch, when the weather is pleasant and perfect for sightseeing.


Taranga Hill

Taranga refers to a three-peaked hill located in the Mehsana district of Gujarat, on the banks of the river Sabarmati. One of the Oldest Jain Temples build by Solanki Dynasty Ruler Kumarpal in the 11th Century is on the Taranga Hills.

Shri Dwarkadish Temple

The Dwarkadhish temple, also known as the Jagat Mandir and occasionally spelled Dwarakadheesh, is a Hindu temple dedicated to Krishna, who is worshiped here by the name Dwarkadhish, or 'King of Dwarka'. The temple is located at Dwarka city of Gujarat, India, which is one of the destinations of Char Dham, a Hindu pilgrimage circuit.According to tradition, the original temple was believed to have been built by Krishna's grandson, Vajranabha, over the hari-griha (Krishna's residential place).

Vadodara

Vadodara is the Second largest city in Gujarat, India.he city is prominent for landmarks such as the Laxmi Vilas Palace, which served as the residence of the Maratha royal Gaekwad dynasty that ruled over Baroda State.Gujarati Thali is famous all over Vadodara for its wide selection and taste of dishes.
